Korea's large metropolitan centers, extensive parks and recreation facilities, high-tech infrastructure, and ultramodern public transit systems are just a few of the factors that have helped Korea become a well-known international business and travel destination. Seoul Olympic Park, located in the capital city of Seoul, preserves the spirit of the 1988 Summer Olympics and is available 24 hours a day, seven days a week, with multipurpose areas for sports, recreation, and a range of cultural activities. South Korea is still one of the most ethnically homogeneous countries in the world. South Koreans are thought to be direct ancestors of various migrating Mongol tribes who arrived on the Korean Peninsula about a half million years ago. South Korea now has a population of around 49 million people, making it one of the world's most densely inhabited countries. South Korea now has diplomatic connections with approximately 170 countries.

Culture

Confucian ideas have had a great influence on Korean society, which affects not only personal life but also business. Confucianism promotes social peace, respect for elders and authority, family, friendship, and ancestors, as well as tradition.

Cuisine

Korean food has developed throughout ages as a result of social and political upheaval. Korean cuisine, which evolved from old agricultural and nomadic practices in Korea and southern Manchuria, exhibits a complex interaction of the natural environment and several cultural influences.
