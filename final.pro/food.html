lets take a look at the far out food of the 70s

The 1970s was a decade that saw significant changes in the way people ate and viewed food. One of the most significant changes was the rise of processed and convenience foods. As more women entered the workforce, there was a growing demand for quick and easy meals that could be prepared in minutes. This led to the popularity of frozen dinners, canned foods, and snack foods such as potato chips and pretzels. In addition to processed and convenience foods, there was also a growing interest in health and wellness during the 70s. This led to an increase in the popularity of natural and organic foods. The first natural food stores began to appear, and people began to embrace whole foods such as fruits, vegetables, and whole grains. There was also a rise in vegetarianism and veganism, with people seeking alternatives to meat and dairy products. When it came to snacks in the 70s, some of the most popular options included Twinkies, Fritos, and Tang. Twinkies were a sweet treat that had been around since the 1930s, but they became even more popular in the 70s. Fritos were a salty snack that was often paired with dips or served with chili, and Tang was a powdered drink mix that was marketed as an alternative to orange juice. Other popular snacks of the era included Pop Rocks, Jell-O, and Reese's Peanut Butter Cups. Overall, the 70s saw a significant shift in the way people ate, with a focus on convenience and health as well as indulgence.

funky fashion

The 70s fashion featured colorful and expressive styles like bell-bottoms and psychedelic prints, with the punk and new wave subcultures emerging later in the decade with leather jackets and ripped clothing.

groovy music

The 70s music was diverse, with iconic bands and artists like Led Zeppelin, Pink Floyd, and the Bee Gees releasing some of their most famous works during the decade. A wide range of genres from disco to punk and new wave emerged during this time, influencing the music industry for years to come.

cars

The 70s brought changes to the automotive industry, including stricter emissions standards and the rise of Japanese imports. Muscle cars like the Ford Mustang remained popular, but fuel-efficient vehicles like the Honda Civic and Toyota Corolla also gained popularity, setting the stage for the future of the industry.
